# SNMR Research, Code, and Experiment Audit

- **Audit time:** 2026-07-16 19:31 UTC
- **Repository head reviewed:** `3a72183`
- **Latest completed experiment:** E39, WBT latent Phase 2, completed 2026-07-16 08:12 UTC
- **Scope:** paper goal, current claims, dirty code, frozen and draft protocols, result artifacts,
  relevant local literature review, and the next baseline/method experiments

## Executive Decision

The project is meaningful, but it currently contains two different research programs:

1. **Main paper:** a shared neural retargeter amortizes a per-robot IK teacher across trained
   humanoids, exposes the cost and limits of sharing, and is validated for contact quality and
   downstream physical tracking.
2. **Follow-up:** the shared latent becomes a command interface for a multi-motion,
   multi-embodiment control policy.

The first program is much closer to a defensible paper. The second has one real positive result:
a G1 WBT policy can track one clip from the frozen latent alone at 72-77% completion across three
training seeds. It has **no tracking-benefit result**. S3 critic preview does not replicate its
promotion threshold, and an explicit-preview critic produces the same seed-0 effect.

The immediate decision is therefore:

- Finish the main paper's missing baselines: calibrated GMR-reference versus SNMR-reference WBT,
  Gate-1b contact-mask closure, and the shared-versus-specialist fidelity intervention.
- Do not launch the full four-arm Phase 3 matrix yet. Calibrate B-multi first.
- Keep L1-multi as a bounded follow-up feasibility test. Do not make S3 a paper claim.
- Reframe the long-term control method around a **load-bearing, control-trained command code**,
  not raw concatenation of the current frozen distillation latent.

## Execution Update - 2026-07-16 20:39 UTC

Gate 1b is now closed as a valid negative result. M1b had support in only 8/42 windows and failed
both stance-speed endpoints. M3 preserved all 98 inherited C4 tensors but produced a 71.5%-dense,
9.6%-precision mask; its projection reached `0.430/0.254 m/s` on the teacher-height/source-contact
coprimaries and raised MPJPE to `6.51 cm`. Per the frozen stop rule, do not iterate masks,
thresholds, or solver weights again. Contact work moves to physics-repaired supervision after a
competent WBT policy exists.

The next active gate is the from-scratch 8k SNMR-reference `walk1_subject5` WBT development
policy. Completion `>=70%` promotes the already frozen three-seed GMR-versus-SNMR confirmatory
matrix; a lower result stops that matrix and triggers reference-failure diagnosis.

The sharing-cost screen is also implementation-ready but remains behind the WBT gate in GPU
priority. Its frozen design gives every robot exactly 50k exposures, compares five same-trainer
specialists against shared base, width-384, and rank-8 per-robot decoder adapters, and promotes
only an arm that closes at least half of both the mean and worst-robot paired gaps within the
registered parameter budgets.

## Review Findings

### P0 - Phase 3 was not launch-ready

The draft assigned 16k PPO iterations to six training clips because it is roughly twice the
single-clip budget. That is not a calibration: each clip receives far less sampling than in the
8k single-clip run, and run/sprint content is not covered by E35's walk calibration. The project
already learned in E34 that an undertrained control makes every downstream comparison
uninterpretable.

The draft arm list also omitted C3 even though E39 triggered its registered inclusion condition.
C3 and S3 have nearly identical seed-0 effects:

| Eval seed | S3 RMSE vs B | C3 RMSE vs B | S3 vs C3 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 404 | -4.4% | -3.7% | -0.66% |
| 405 | -4.9% | -5.1% | +0.16% |

The direct paired per-window intervals cross zero. S3 cannot be called a latent-specific effect.

**Action taken:** the draft now requires B-multi checkpoint calibration at 4k/8k/16k before
latent arms, and the source driver includes C3. The full driver remains infrastructure rather
than a launch-frozen protocol.

### P0 - The latent result is feasibility, not improvement

E39 is valid evidence that the frozen latent can carry a command on one deterministic clip:
L1 completes 72-77% in all six train/eval cells. Relative to explicit commands, however, it loses
8-18 completion points and 5-14% survival; RMSE is worse for training seeds 0 and 1 and tied only
for seed 2.

S3 is strongly seed-dependent:

| Train seed | Completion delta | RMSE delta | Survival delta |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 0 | +2.5 pp | -4.6% | +2.9% |
| 1 | +0.5 pp | -0.1% | +0.2% |
| 2 | +5.5 pp | -12.0% | +5.9% |

The median seed misses the frozen 5%/5 pp threshold. The correct paper language is:

> A frozen SNMR latent supports single-clip WBT as a load-bearing offline command, but it has
> not improved tracking over explicit robot-space commands.

### P0 - Recent WBT work does not answer the main paper's downstream question

E36-E39 keep the **GMR reference fixed** for rewards, termination, and evaluation. They test the
latent interface, not whether SNMR-retargeted motion is as physically trackable as GMR motion.
Paper claim C6 still needs a sufficiently trained, matched GMR-reference versus SNMR-reference
comparison. E34 cannot answer it because both controls had 0% ten-second completion.

This is now the highest-value missing baseline. A positive non-inferiority result supports the
retargeter paper directly; a negative result identifies the physical cost of amortization.

### P1 - Phase 2 analysis under-enforced its artifact contract

The frozen analyzer accepted incomplete or duplicate manifests, did not enforce the expected
20-cell matrix, and took a median over six train/eval cells instead of first aggregating the two
evaluation seeds within each training seed. Evaluation seeds measure rollout variability; they
are not additional independently trained policies.

The completed artifact contains all expected cells, and manual inspection confirms the intended
actor/critic functions, motion files, seeds, 8k iteration budget, and 1,024 environments in every
Holosoma config. Reanalysis by training seed preserves `replicated:l1`.

**Action taken:** source analyzers now reject missing/duplicate matrices and report per-training-
seed summaries. Frozen run copies and frozen result JSONs were not rewritten.

Residual provenance gap: the Phase 2 manifest does not itself carry checkpoint hashes or
observation functions. Future drivers should put both in the analyzed manifest and validate the
saved Holosoma config, as the Phase-0 analyzer already does.

### P1 - M1b's smoke did not test the projection solver

`runs/gate1b/smoke_m1b.json` has zero selected stance samples and returns
`empty_contact_support`. Reproduction shows that full-clip decoded foot minima are 5.1/6.9 cm
below the first-window minima, exceeding the 3 cm onset threshold. This may be a real failure of
clip-minimum normalization under decoder vertical drift, but it is not a successful plumbing
smoke.

Run a support-only audit across the frozen 42 windows first. Then use a support-bearing window
for solver plumbing and retain zero-support windows as failures in the full result. Do not tune
the frozen solver.

### P1 - "Preview" is offline and noncausal

The exported `z_t` is produced by encoding an entire clip with an unmasked Transformer. The
current checkpoint also has no positional encoding. Thus `z_t` already mixes content from other
frames, and its future deltas are not equivalent to a causal future-reference encoder.

This is acceptable for offline motion-reference tracking, but it blocks claims about live
teleoperation, causal anticipation, or deployable current-frame semantics. A future command
paper needs a causal/windowed encoder with an explicit horizon contract.

### P1 - Phase 3's held-out wording needs precision

`walk1_subject5` is excluded from Phase-3 policy training, but it selected the observation
design, thresholds, and replication path in E36-E39. It is **policy-held-out but
researcher-development-seen**. `run2_subject1` is the cleaner primary clip.

For a generalization claim, reserve at least one additional SNMR validation clip before looking
at any Phase-3 latent result. Report results by clip; do not hide a hard-clip collapse in a
two-clip mean.

### P2 - Generated artifacts were about to enter Git

The dirty tree included roughly 459 MB of regenerable multi-clip NPZs and hundreds of MB of raw
PPO logs. These are not reviewable scientific records. Protocols, hashes, manifests, compact
reports, and analyses belong in Git; NPZs, checkpoints, and raw training/eval logs do not.

**Action taken:** the ignore rules now cover the new latent experiment directories without
discarding compact result artifacts.

## Paper Goal

The strongest honest goal is:

> **SNMR amortizes per-robot optimization into one embodiment-conditioned neural retargeter for
> multiple trained humanoids, and quantifies what sharing preserves, what it costs, and what
> physical correction remains necessary for downstream control.**

This goal is narrower than zero-shot universal retargeting and stronger than "we trained one
network." It naturally supports four contributions:

1. **Amortized multi-embodiment retargeting:** one model, five trained robots, explicit runtime
   and fidelity comparison to GMR and matched specialists.
2. **Measured representation sharing:** high paired alignment/retrieval together with high
   nonlinear embodiment leakage. Use "aligned, not invariant."
3. **Physical-error diagnosis:** small pose error differentiates into large stance velocity;
   soft contact objectives fail, while oracle-mask projection localizes the remaining problem.
4. **Downstream validation:** calibrated WBT tests whether amortized references are physically
   non-inferior to the optimization teacher.

Claims that remain unsupported:

- zero-shot decoding to unseen robots;
- positive transfer from sharing;
- contact-consistent raw network output;
- improved WBT from SNMR references;
- a semantic or contact-organized latent;
- causal latent control;
- latent critic benefit.

The current abstract should not add E39 as a tracking contribution. At most, put latent-command
feasibility in an appendix or future-work section unless Phase 3 produces held-out evidence.

## Baseline Plan

### B1 - Calibrated downstream baseline, highest priority

Freeze a current-revision walk1 development protocol before training SNMR-reference policies:

- Sources: GMR reference and SNMR decoded reference.
- Same robot, clip, simulator, rewards, termination, initialization, policy architecture,
  environment count, train seed, and eval starts.
- Start with one 8k SNMR development policy because GMR reaches 88% completion at 8k.
- If SNMR completion is at least 70%, run a clean confirmatory matrix with both sources trained
  from scratch for seeds {0,1,2}, eval seeds {404,405}.
- Primary: completion non-inferiority margin -5 pp.
- Coprimary: joint-RMSE relative margin +10%.
- Secondary: survival, body/root errors, contacts, torque, power, and per-phase failures.

Do not reuse a mix of continuation and from-scratch checkpoints in the confirmatory matrix.
After walk1, calibrate a second clip using GMR only before comparing sources.

### B2 - Contact-mask closure

1. Audit M1b support/precision/recall on all 42 frozen windows.
2. Run the full deterministic M1b study unchanged.
3. If it fails for low support/precision, run the already registered M3 teacher-height-label
   contact-head retrain.
4. If M3 fails, close mask iteration and move to physics-repaired supervision.

Success means both speed endpoints and every physical guard pass. Anything else remains a
negative result, not a tunable near miss.

### B3 - Sharing-cost intervention

The shared model's matched G1 gap is a core-paper weakness. Run a small, attributable screen:

| Arm | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Specialist matched exposure | lower-bound control |
| Current shared model matched exposure | baseline |
| Wider shared trunk | capacity test |
| Shared trunk + small per-robot decoder adapters | conditional-capacity test |

Keep robot exposures and LR progress matched. Promote only an arm that closes at least half the
specialist gap on mean and worst-robot MPJPE without more than roughly 20% robot-specific
parameters. Test one seed first, then replicate only the winner.

## Algorithm and Framework Roadmap

### Track A - Main-paper method

1. **Current shared encoder + decoder:** preserve as the measured baseline.
2. **Conditional decoder capacity:** use low-rank/residual per-robot adapters or stronger
   layer-wise embodiment conditioning. This directly attacks the sharing gap.
3. **Morphology-complete features:** add joint limits, link scale, mass/inertia, actuator limits,
   and contact geometry. The current eight static features are too weak for a serious unseen-
   embodiment claim.
4. **Few-shot adaptation before zero-shot:** train on synthetic morphology variants and evaluate
   adapter calibration on multiple held-out robots. One failed LORO robot is not a sufficient
   generalization study.
5. **Hybrid physical correction:** keep the network as amortizer and use a bounded projection or
   repaired supervision for the residual physical constraints.

### Track B - Latent-command follow-up

The current `z` is optimized for teacher reconstruction and cross-embodiment alignment. E16 and
E38 show that category/contact structure is not exposed linearly. A control representation
should be trained for control:

1. Replace full-clip content attention with a causal or fixed-window encoder carrying an explicit
   time horizon and positional encoding.
2. Add predictive objectives for future canonical keypoints/root velocity and supervised contact
   phase. Keep reconstruction/alignment as auxiliary losses.
3. Compress through a policy adapter and inject the command with FiLM/gating or a dedicated
   command encoder; do not concatenate 384 raw channels into a generic MLP.
4. Add an offline command-decoding control: `z + embodiment -> robot q/qdot`. This separates
   information loss in `z` from PPO's difficulty learning the decoder.
5. Only then train a shared multi-robot policy with a shared trunk and small robot action
   adapters. The embodiment code must affect both command decoding and control.

Required controls for any latent claim:

- explicit robot command;
- phase/time code with matched width;
- current latent without deltas;
- explicit future preview;
- latent future preview;
- latent-only command;
- matched parameter count or a compact adapter.

## Experiment Sequence and Stop Rules

| Order | Experiment | Decision |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | M1b support audit + full M1b | pass contact or trigger M3 |
| 2 | GMR-vs-SNMR 8k walk1 development WBT | decide whether confirmatory source study is viable |
| 3 | M3 if triggered | pass or close mask iteration |
| 4 | Three-seed GMR-vs-SNMR WBT | establish downstream non-inferiority/failure |
| 5 | Width vs adapter sharing-cost screen | choose main-method improvement |
| 6 | B-multi budget calibration | establish Phase-3 horizon |
| 7 | B-multi vs L1-multi | test held-out latent-command feasibility |
| 8 | S3/C3 multi only if still scientifically useful | attribution side study, not main path |

Stop rules:

- No more actor-concatenated S1/S2 runs without a new architecture or objective.
- Close S3 if multi-clip S3 and C3 remain indistinguishable.
- Close frozen-latent control if L1-multi collapses below 40% or widens the single-clip
  completion gap on the clean held-out clip.
- Do not attempt shared multi-robot WBT until L1-multi tracks at least two held-out motions.
- Do not reopen solver weights inside Gate 1b.
- Do not claim zero-shot embodiment generalization before multi-holdout, morphology-diverse
  evaluation.

## Literature Synthesis

The local review supports the direction but also explains the negative results:

- UniTracker (arXiv:2507.07356) and VMP support making a latent load-bearing; UniTracker also
  reports interference when explicit references make the latent redundant.
- GMT (arXiv:2506.14770) supports future context, but its encoder is trained with the controller;
  it is not evidence that a frozen retargeting latent should help by concatenation.
- FLD (arXiv:2402.13820) and MaskedMimic support task-aligned latent decoding/feasibility, not an
  unchanged frozen auxiliary feature.
- Retargeting Matters (arXiv:2510.02252) and OmniRetarget (arXiv:2509.26633) reinforce that
  reference quality and contact preservation are first-order for downstream WBT.
- Representation-RL evaluations are seed-sensitive; E39 empirically confirms that warning.

Novelty language should remain "we found no prior work with this exact frozen-auxiliary setup"
until a reproducible literature-search appendix exists. Do not write "first" from the current
narrative review alone.

## Final Map

The project has a clear primary path:

```text
human motion
  -> shared SNMR amortizer
  -> embodiment-conditioned robot motion
  -> deployable contact correction when required
  -> calibrated WBT validation
```

The longer-term framework is:

```text
human/robot motion
  -> causal, control-trained shared command code
  + morphology-complete embodiment code
  -> command decoder / gated shared controller
  -> small robot action adapter
  -> physics feedback for feasibility and refinement
```

The first path can support the current paper. The second is promising, but E39 says it should be
developed as a load-bearing control representation rather than presented as a benefit of the
current frozen latent.
